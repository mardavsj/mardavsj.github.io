Common Obstacle Types

Spike Traps

Stationary or moving spikes that instantly destroy balloons on contact.

Avoidance: Maintain safe distance and time movements around spike patterns.

Moving Platforms

Platforms that move in patterns, requiring timed jumps to board safely.

Timing: Study platform movement cycles for perfect boarding opportunities.

Opening Gates

Gates that open and close in regular patterns, creating narrow time windows.

Precision: Time passage through gates requires observation of cycle timing.

Dead End Traps

Paths that appear valid but lead to inescapable traps or loops.

Recognition: Learn to identify dead end indicators before committing to paths.

Wind Currents

Invisible forces that push balloons off course or into obstacles.

Compensation: Learn to counteract wind effects with precise counter-movements.

Time Pressure

Levels with countdown timers requiring fast completion.

Speed: Time pressure levels demand pre-planned routes and flawless execution.
